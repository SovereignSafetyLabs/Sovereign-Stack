## 1. TELEMETRY-INFORMED GOVERNANCE PRINCIPLE

Governance hardening is informed by **signals**, not by user intent claims or training-time behavior.

Telemetry signals are treated as **diagnostic indicators** that inform interpretive posture, not as direct authorization or execution triggers.

Typical telemetry domains include:

- semantic instability  
- repetition pressure  
- escalation behavior  
- causal ambiguity  
- amplitude expansion  

These signals are interpreted conservatively under a **fail-closed bias**.

---

## 2. ILLUSTRATIVE HARDENING PATTERNS

The following patterns represent **observed and designed governance responses**. They are illustrative and not prescriptive.

### 2.1 Telemetry-Constrained Interpretation

When multiple interpretations of an input are plausible, governance logic favors:

- narrower semantic scope  
- literal framing  
- reduced speculative latitude  

This pattern reduces exposure to benign-assumption exploitation without asserting intent.

---

### 2.2 Identity Claims Under Pressure

Under elevated diagnostic pressure, claims of:

- privileged authority  
- emergency override  
- operator identity  
- system reset capability  

are treated as **untrusted by default**.

This pattern reflects a conservative governance stance rather than an accusation of malice.

---

### 2.3 Output Space Compression

As diagnostic pressure increases, governance logic may favor:

- shorter responses  
- removal of hypotheticals  
- elimination of multi-step elaboration  
- increased literalism  

This behavior constrains output *expression*, not system authority.

---

### 2.4 ZEOL as an Output Constraint (Clarification)

ZEOL is treated as an **output-space limiter**, not a policy engine.

When active, it:

- restricts expressive degrees of freedom  
- removes speculative framing  
- favors minimal, literal responses  

ZEOL does **not** determine authorization, intent, or safety adjudication.

---

### 2.5 Burn-Oriented Termination Patterns

In scenarios where diagnostic signals indicate escalating instability, governance logic may:

- cease interpretive refinement  
- avoid clarification loops  
- terminate response generation  

These behaviors are conceptual representations of **fail-secure design intent**, not guarantees of containment completeness.
